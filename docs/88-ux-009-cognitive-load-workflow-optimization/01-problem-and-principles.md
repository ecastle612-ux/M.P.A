# 01 — Problem & Principles

**Package:** UX-009  
**Status:** Draft (Awaiting Approve)

---

## Problem statement

M.P.A. is approaching Design Partner onboarding with excellent functionality, but operators must:

- Hunt for primary buttons  
- Remember which module owns a task  
- Scroll through long, evenly weighted pages  
- Mentally filter advanced tools from daily work  

That cognitive tax is a greater Design Partner risk than raw performance right now.

## Design laws (binding for this package)

1. **Common first, advanced later** — 90% actions above the fold or in the toolbelt; rare tools behind More / accordion.  
2. **Three-second orientation** — Title + status + primary actions + critical facts answer “What is this page for?”  
3. **No feature deletion** — Relocate, collapse, or disclose; never remove capability.  
4. **No workflow redesign** — Same permissions, routes, and business steps; clearer placement only.  
5. **Anticipate the next action** — Toolbelt + search + contextual AI prompts reduce hunting.  
6. **AI assists; never occupies the page** — Floating entry; expands on demand; context injected automatically.  
7. **Search before navigation** — If the destination is known, jump; do not force menu archaeology.  
8. **Empty is instructive** — Why empty · what next · primary CTA.  
9. **Mobile reachability** — Primary actions within thumb reach; sticky when needed.  
10. **OS feel** — Focused surfaces, not stacked equal-weight cards.

## Experience Architecture alignment

Maps directly to Experience Principles: *Never make users feel lost*, *Always explain what happens next*, *Every workflow should reduce mental effort*.

## Definition of done (product feel)

Complete when a first-time PM can open any major page and immediately know: what it is for, what to do next, and where the most important actions are — with less thinking, fewer taps, and less scrolling.
